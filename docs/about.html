About the Summer School

The summer school is intended for researchers who have basic familiarity with the experimental technologies and their applications in biology, and who are interested in making the step from a user of bioinformatics software towards adapting or developing their own analysis workflows.

The four practical sessions of the summer school will require you to follow and modify scripts in the computer language R.

Topics Covered

The programme covers a wide range of topics, including but not limited to:

  • Introduction to R and Bioconductor
  • Elements of statistics: visualization, regression, PCA and other low-dimensional embeddings, statistical tests incl. multiple, classification / supervised machine learning
  • RNA-Seq data analysis – both bulk and single-cell
  • Spatial omics and annotation workflows – genes, genomic features, variants, transcripts, and proteins
  • Mass spectrometry-based proteomics and metabolomics
  • Immunobioinformatics
  • Experimental design, batch effects, and confounding
  • Reproducible research and workflow authoring with R Markdown
  • Package development, version control, and developer tools

Summer School Structure

The programme is structured to provide a balanced mix of theory and hands-on practice, spread across five days:

  • Morning lectures
    20 sessions of 45 minutes each
    📅 Monday to Friday — 🕣 08:30–12:00

  • Practical computer tutorials
    Interactive lab sessions in small groups
    📅 Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday — 🕐 13:30–16:30

  • Flashlight talks
    Short presentations by participants
    📅 Monday, Tuesday, Thursday — 🕟 16:30–17:00

  • Evening sessions
    Informal discussions, Q&A and project groups
    📅 Monday and Tuesday — 🕗 20:30–22:00

  • Midweek mountain hike
    Social and cultural activity in the Alps
    📅 Wednesday — 🕑 14:00–22:00

Teaching Materials

All lecture slides and workshop materials will be provided during the summer school and will remain accessible after the event. Participants are encouraged to actively engage with the content and explore additional resources shared by the instructors.

Computer Tutorials

During the hands-on labs, you will work at your own pace in small groups, with expert guidance provided by morning lecturers and dedicated teaching assistants.

What to prepare

Please bring your own laptop with:

  • The latest version of R (4.3.x)
  • Bioconductor 3.18 installed
  • Admin rights on your machine
  • ≥ 8 GB RAM
  • ≥ 20 GB free disk space
Important note

Please make sure to set up your system before arrival.
Internet connections at the venue may be slow or unreliable.

Social Programme

One afternoon is dedicated to a joint cultural and outdoor activity.
We plan a trip into the mountains with a light hike in the high-alpine area (weather permitting), followed by a delicious local dinner.
